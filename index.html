About Me

As a linguist and cognitive scientist, I have been primarily interested in how people represent and access langauge in memory. When people hear another dialect, how do they know it is different? Where do people think different dialects are spoken? Are people able to accurately identify where someone is from based on how they speak? How do people classify their own dialect in relation to others?

More generally, I am interested in how people's mental representations of the world affect the way they interact with people and objects. I have a great deal of experience in conducting human subjects research in the field and in a laboratory setting. Throughout my career, I have conducted research projects from end-to-end, from concept through experiment design, participant recruitment, data management and analysis, and presentation of results. I have also managed and mentored a number of research assistants along the way.

I hold a PhD in Linguistics from Indiana University with a minor in Cognitive Science and an MA in English from Oklahoma State University with specialties in Linguistics and Teaching English as a Second Language.
